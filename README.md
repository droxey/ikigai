## The Intersections

### 🔥 Passion (Love ∩ Good At)

I genuinely enjoy building things and then figuring out how to teach others to build them too. Whether it’s deploying infrastructure, tinkering with hardware, or dialing in a cocktail recipe — I tend to approach everything with the same curiosity and care. I just like understanding how things work, and I get a lot of satisfaction from sharing what I learn.

### 🌊 Mission (Love ∩ Needs)

I care a lot about making technology feel human and approachable. My hackathons put students in a video game world to code together. BumpBand tries to replace phone fumbling with a simple wrist bump. My courses anchor every topic to a destination project so students can see where they’re headed. I think the best technology brings people together rather than isolating them.

### 💎 Profession (Good At ∩ Paid For)

I’ve been lucky enough to work across a lot of domains — architecting infrastructure, auditing codebases, prototyping hardware, writing curriculum, building interview systems. Most people specialize, and I respect that. I just happened to go wide and deep, and I’m grateful the dots connected. I try to bring that breadth to everything I build and teach.

### 🎯 Vocation (Paid For ∩ Needs)

The projects I’m most excited about come from problems I’ve actually experienced. Team Vault started because I kept struggling to save web content for my repos. BumpBand came from wanting a simpler way to exchange contacts in person. FieldOps grew out of watching students struggle with the gap between tutorials and real work. I’m not sure any of these will be huge — but they’re honest attempts to solve real problems I care about.
